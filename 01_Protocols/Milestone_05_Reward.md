# 🦅 Milestone 5: Identity Reinforcement (Make it Satisfying)

> "What is immediately rewarded is repeated. What is immediately punished is avoided." — James Clear

## 🔬 The Science: The Immediate Reward Loop
Habits often have "Delayed Rewards" (e.g., losing weight takes months). To make a habit stick, we must attach an **Immediate Reward**. The best reward is the feeling of **Identity Alignment**.

---

## 🟢 Slow Track: The Identity Vote
**Duration: 30 Seconds | Focus: Internal Reward**

### 1. The Affirmation
Immediately after your 2-minute habit, perform the "Identity Check."
*"That’s like me. I am the type of person who [Identity]."*

### 2. The Dopamine Hit
Acknowledge the win. You didn't just "read a page"; you **cast a vote** for being a reader.

---

## 🟡 Fast Track: Visual Tracking & The Social Contract
**Duration: 15 Minutes | Focus: External Reinforcement**

### 1. The Visual Strike (Don't Break the Chain)
Use a calendar or a physical tracker. Every day you complete the habit, mark a large "X." The visual progress becomes a reward in itself.

### 2. The "Never Miss Twice" Rule
A single miss is an accident. Two misses in a row is the start of a **new habit**.
- *Task*: If you miss a day, your ONLY goal for tomorrow is to show up for 2 minutes. No excuses.

### 3. The Habit Contract
Find an Accountability Partner.
- **The Contract**: "If I miss [Habit] for more than [X] days, I will [Penalty]."
- *Example: I will pay my partner $20.*
- *Example: I will do the dishes for a week.*

### 4. The Goldilocks Check
Is the habit getting too boring? Increase the difficulty (Move to Phase 2 of your Gateway Map).
Is the habit getting too hard? Decrease the difficulty (Move back to Phase 1).
