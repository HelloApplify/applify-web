# 🦅 Milestone 2: The Awareness Engine (Awareness)

> "Until you make the unconscious conscious, it will direct your life and you will call it fate." — Carl Jung

## 🔬 The Science: The Basal Ganglia & Autopilot
Your habits are stored in the **Basal Ganglia**, a part of the brain that requires almost zero conscious energy. This is why you can drive home without "remembering" the drive. To change a habit, we must pull it out of the Basal Ganglia and back into the **Prefrontal Cortex**.

---

## 🟢 Slow Track: The Point-and-Call Audit
**Duration: 10 Minutes | Focus: Recognition**

### 1. The Scripting
List the first 5 habits of your morning and the last 5 of your night.
- *Morning*: 1. ________ 2. ________ 3. ________ 4. ________ 5. ________
- *Night*: 1. ________ 2. ________ 3. ________ 4. ________ 5. ________

### 2. The Labeling (+) (-) (=)
Mark each one. 
- **(+) Positive**: Helps your identity (e.g., meditation).
- **(-) Negative**: Hurts your identity (e.g., checking social media in bed).
- **(=) Neutral**: Just happens (e.g., drinking water).

### 3. The Anchor Identification
Which habit on your list is "Un-breakable"? We will use this in Milestone 3.

## 🟡 Fast Track: The 24-Hour Habit Scorecard
**Duration: 1 Day | Focus: Total Diagnostic**

### 1. The Log
For one full day, use the Point-and-Call method for *every* action.
- "I'm opening the fridge."
- "I'm sitting on the couch."
- "I'm checking my phone."

### 2. The Friction Audit
Identify the "Negative" habits that occur most frequently. Trace them back to a physical location.
- *Example: I check my phone (Negative) whenever I'm in the kitchen (Location).*

### 3. The Dead Time Search
Find at least 30 minutes of "Neutral" or "Negative" time that can be reclaimed for your new identity.
