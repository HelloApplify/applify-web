# 🦅 Milestone 3: Cue Architecture (Make it Obvious)

> "Environment is the invisible hand that shapes human behavior." — James Clear

## 🔬 The Science: Context is the Cue
Your brain doesn't just see "a couch"; it sees "the place where I watch Netflix." This is a **Contextual Cue**. To build a new habit, you must either find a **new space** or **prime the current space** so that the cue for the new habit is un-ignorable.

---

## 🟢 Slow Track: The Simple Habit Stack
**Duration: 5 Minutes | Focus: Connection**

### 1. The Implementation Intention
Research shows that being specific about **Time** and **Location** doubles your odds.
*"I will [HABIT] at [TIME] in [LOCATION]."*

### 2. The Habit Stack
Use your Anchor from Milestone 2:
*"After I [CURRENT HABIT], I will [NEW HABIT]."*

- **Task**: Write your stack on a physical sticky note. Place it at the "Point of Action" (e.g., on the bathroom mirror).

---

## 🟡 Fast Track: Environment Choice Architecture
**Duration: 45 Minutes | Focus: Visual Engineering**

### 1. The Visual Audit
Walk through your house. For your target habit, ask: "Can I see the cue within 2 seconds of entering the room?"
- *If No*: You must move the cue. (e.g., Put the guitar in the middle of the room).

### 2. The "Choice Architecture" Redesign
Make the "Good" choice the easiest one:
- **Hydration**: Place a water bottle in every room you frequent.
- **Reading**: Place a book on your pillow every morning when you make the bed.
- **Gym**: Lay your workout clothes out the night before.

### 3. The "Curbing" Rule
Make the "Bad" choice harder:
- **Phone Addiction**: Put the charger in a room you don't frequent.
- **Junk Food**: Move snacks to a high shelf or inside a drawer.
